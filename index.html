The diagnosis

Does this sound familiar?

If you identify with at least two of these problems, I can help.

Your operation lives in Excel

Shared spreadsheets emailed around that nobody knows which version is correct. Data gets lost, duplicated, or corrupted. If the person managing "the master Excel" goes on vacation, the process stops.

Your approvals run on email or WhatsApp

Approval processes depend on someone reading an email or message. No traceability, no measurable timelines, no automatic escalation. Requests get lost in inboxes.

Your reports are built manually every week

Someone exports data from a system, pastes it in Excel, formats it, and sends a PDF by email. Every week. And still, the numbers don't always add up.

You have no real-time visibility

To know how the business is doing today, you have to ask, wait, and trust the answer is correct. No dashboard, no updated data, no certainty.

Your systems don't talk to each other

Data in SQL Server, tickets in a service desk, documents in SharePoint, tracking in Excel. Nothing connects. Data is fragmented and nobody has the complete picture.

You depend on a key person

If that person goes on vacation, gets sick, or quits, the process stops. No documentation, no system. Just knowledge in someone's head.

AI-Powered Technical Assistant

From searching manuals for hours to finding answers in seconds

Problem

Field technicians wasted significant time searching for technical documentation. They navigated SharePoint folders without an intelligent search system, sent messages to colleagues asking for files, or resolved issues without consulting official documentation.

Solution

A Power Apps app where the technician types their query in natural language. AI Builder extracts keywords, builds a KQL query against SharePoint Search API, filters results by the technician's area/role, and returns relevant documents in seconds.

Results

About me

I started my career in infrastructure and technical support. That's where I learned something many technologists never understand: technology is worthless if it doesn't solve a real business problem.

Over time, I moved toward automation and data management. I saw firsthand how entire companies operated with spreadsheets shared by email, WhatsApp approvals, reports built manually every Monday, and critical processes that depended on "someone remembering" to do something.

That's where I found my professional mission: eliminating that operational fragility.

Microsoft Power Platform became my main tool because it allows you to take a broken process and turn it into a complete digital system — with interface, database, automation, approvals and reporting — without needing months of custom development or half-million dollar budgets.
